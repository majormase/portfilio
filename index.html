6 Questions For Minimalist Design

By Drew Griffin

Minimal interior design style gained momentum in the mid-2000s. This style is characterized by simplicity, clarity and functionality of every element with nothing extraneous; it could be seen as the realization that ease of use was not tied to creating a vision of physical reality, in fact it was often the opposite as performance limits and clutter was pushed to the maximum. And perhaps the primary catalyst for this push has been the integration of smaller spaces. Economic living and the desire for white space has minimalism in higher demand than ever.
